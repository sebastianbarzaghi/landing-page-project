A Romance of Many Dimensions
1884 novella by Edwin Abbott Abbott.
Some random information
The book used the fictional two-dimensional world of Flatland to comment on the hierarchy of Victorian culture through its examination of dimensions.
The story describes a two-dimensional world inhabited by geometric figures; women are line segments, while men are polygons with various numbers of sides.
The first half of the story goes through the practicalities of existing in a two-dimensional universe, as well as a history leading up to the year 1999 on the eve of the 3rd Millennium.
In the world of Flatland, classes are distinguished by the "Art of Hearing", the "Art of Feeling", and the "Art of Sight Recognition".
An unspeakable horror seized me. There was a darkness; then a dizzy, sickening sensation of sight that was not like seeing; I saw a Line that was no Line; Space that was not Space: I was myself, and not myself. When I could find voice, I shrieked aloud in agony, "Either this is madness or it is Hell."
